Aligarh Muslim University (abbreviated as AMU) is a public central university in Aligarh, Uttar Pradesh, India,
which was originally established by Sir Syed Ahmad Khan as the Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College in 1875.
Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College became Aligarh Muslim University in 1920, following the Aligarh Muslim University
Act. It has three off-campus centres in AMU Malappuram Campus (Kerala), AMU Murshidabad Centre (West Bengal), 
and Kishanganj Centre (Bihar).

The university offers more than 300 courses in traditional and modern branches of education, and is an institute 
of national importance as declared under seventh schedule of the Constitution of India at its commencement.
